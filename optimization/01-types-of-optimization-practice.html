Types of Optimization

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. For a convex function, a local minimum is…
Convexity guarantees it's global.
Always, not sometimes, for convex $f$.
✅ Correct — no traps in a convex bowl: local = global.
A min isn't a max.
Q2. "Maximize $g$" is the same as…
That's the opposite goal.
✅ Correct — $\max g=-\min(-g)$.
That flips it the wrong way.
There is a standard equivalence.
Q3. "$\min f(x)$ subject to $x\ge0$" is…
There's a restriction, so it's constrained.
✅ Correct — the condition $x\ge0$ is a constraint.
Convexity depends on $f$, not the constraint alone.
It's a perfectly valid problem.
Q4. $f(x)=x^2$ is…
An upward parabola is convex.
✅ Correct — a single upward bowl is convex.
Concave is the upside-down version.
It's quadratic, not linear.
Q5. A non-convex landscape can trap you in a…
The global min is the goal, not a trap.
Constraints define the region, not a trap valley.
✅ Correct — extra valleys mean you can stall in a local min.
It's about local minima, not maxima.
Q6. The objective function is also called the…
Constraints restrict $x$; they're separate.
✅ Correct — objective = cost / loss we minimize.
The gradient is its slope, not the function.
That's the set of allowed $x$.
Q7. A sum of two convex functions is…
Convexity is preserved under addition.
Adding convex functions stays convex.
✅ Correct — a sum of convex functions is convex.
It's well-defined and convex.
Q8. "Convex function" vs "convex set" — they describe…
They're related but distinct concepts.
Both are central to optimization.
✅ Correct — function shape (bowl) vs feasible region shape.
Neither is about max vs min directly.
