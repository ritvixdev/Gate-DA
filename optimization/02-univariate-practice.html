Univariate Optimization

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. At a stationary point, $f''(x)\gt0$ indicates a…
A max needs $f''\lt0$.
✅ Correct — positive curvature → local minimum.
Saddles are multivariate.
It's decisive.
Q2. Minimize $f(x)=x^2-6x+10$. $x^*=?$
That's $-b$, not $-b/(2a)$.
Sign error — $-b/(2a)=6/2=3$.
✅ Correct — $f'=2x-6=0\Rightarrow x=3$.
That's the constant term.
Q3. Vertex of $3x^2-12x+1$ is at…
Sign — it's $-b/(2a)=12/6=2$.
✅ Correct — $x=-b/(2a)=12/6=2$ (a min since $a\gt0$).
That's $-b$, not divided by $2a$.
Divide by $2a=6$, giving 2.
Q4. $f'(x_0)=0$ and $f''(x_0)=-4$. The point is a…
A min needs $f''\gt0$.
✅ Correct — negative curvature → maximum.
Inflection is when $f''=0$.
It's a max.
Q5. If $f''(x_0)=0$, the second-derivative test is…
Can't conclude min from $f''=0$.
Can't conclude max either.
✅ Correct — inconclusive; use sign changes / higher derivatives (e.g. $x^4$ is a min).
Not necessarily — $x^4$ at 0 is a min.
Q6. The necessary condition for an interior optimum of smooth $f$ is…
That's the curvature, used to classify.
✅ Correct — the slope must be zero at an interior optimum.
The function value being zero is unrelated.
The optimum needn't be at the origin.
Q7. $f(x)=x^3-3x$: how many stationary points?
$f'=3x^2-3=0$ has two roots.
✅ Correct — $x=\pm1$ (a min and a max).
Only $x=\pm1$ solve $f'=0$.
There are two.
Q8. On a closed interval, the optimum can occur…
Endpoints can win too.
No reason it's the center.
✅ Correct — check interior stationary points and the endpoints.
A continuous function on a closed interval attains its optimum.
