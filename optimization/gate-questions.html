GATE DA 2024 Univariate optimization
Q1. For any twice-differentiable function $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$, if at some $x_0$ we have $f'(x_0)=0$ and $f''(x_0)>0$, then $f$ necessarily has a ______ at $x=x_0$.
✓ Correct — $f'(x_0)=0$ makes $x_0$ stationary and $f''(x_0)>0$ means it curves upward: a local minimum (the second-derivative test).
The test is local; it cannot guarantee the point is the global minimum.
A maximum needs $f''(x_0)<0$.
Only local information is given, and $f''>0$ indicates a minimum, not a maximum.
