­ƒÄ▓ Probability & Statistics

Rules, distributions and all four hypothesis tests ÔÇö condensed for last-week revision.

1 ┬À Probability rules
$$P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B) \qquad P(A^c)=1-P(A)$$
$$P(A\mid B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)} \qquad \text{Independent: } P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)\ \Leftrightarrow\ P(A\mid B)=P(A)$$

Mutually exclusive ($P(A\cap B)=0$) is not independence ÔÇö exclusive events with non-zero probabilities are always dependent.

2 ┬À Total probability & Bayes
$$P(B)=\sum_i P(B\mid A_i)P(A_i) \qquad P(A_k\mid B)=\frac{P(B\mid A_k)P(A_k)}{\sum_i P(B\mid A_i)P(A_i)}$$

Disease-test template: $P(D\mid +)=\dfrac{\text{sens}\cdot p}{\text{sens}\cdot p+\text{FPR}\cdot(1-p)}$ ÔÇö small prevalence $p$ makes this surprisingly small even for good tests.

3 ┬À Random variables, expectation, variance
$$E[X]=\sum x\,p(x)\ \text{or}\ \int x f(x)dx \qquad \operatorname{Var}(X)=E[X^2]-(E[X])^2$$
$$E[aX+b]=aE[X]+b \qquad \operatorname{Var}(aX+b)=a^2\operatorname{Var}(X)$$

PMF: $\sum p(x)=1$. PDF: $f(x)\ge0$, $\int f=1$, $P(X=x)=0$ for continuous $X$. CDF $F(x)=P(X\le x)$, non-decreasing, $F(-\infty)=0$, $F(\infty)=1$; for continuous $X$, $f=F'$.

$E[X+Y]=E[X]+E[Y]$ always; $\operatorname{Var}(X\pm Y)=\operatorname{Var}X+\operatorname{Var}Y\pm2\operatorname{Cov}(X,Y)$ (covariance term drops iff uncorrelated).

5 ┬À Joint distributions, covariance, correlation
$$\operatorname{Cov}(X,Y)=E[XY]-E[X]E[Y] \qquad \rho=\frac{\operatorname{Cov}(X,Y)}{\sigma_X\sigma_Y}\in[-1,1]$$

Marginal: $p_X(x)=\sum_y p(x,y)$. Independent $\Rightarrow$ $\operatorname{Cov}=0$, but $\operatorname{Cov}=0\;\not\Rightarrow$ independent (classic trap; holds only for jointly normal). $\operatorname{Cov}(aX,bY)=ab\operatorname{Cov}(X,Y)$, $\operatorname{Cov}(X,X)=\operatorname{Var}(X)$.

6 ┬À Descriptive statistics
$$\bar x=\frac1n\sum x_i \qquad s^2=\frac{1}{n-1}\sum(x_i-\bar x)^2 \qquad \text{IQR}=Q_3-Q_1$$

Median: middle value (robust to outliers); mode: most frequent. Box plot whisker fences: $Q_1-1.5\,\text{IQR}$ and $Q_3+1.5\,\text{IQR}$ ÔÇö beyond = outliers. Right-skewed: mean > median; left-skewed: mean < median. Histograms show shape; box plots compare spread/outliers.

7 ┬À Sampling & CLT
$$\bar X\;\xrightarrow{\ n\ \text{large}\ }\;N\!\Big(\mu,\ \frac{\sigma^2}{n}\Big) \qquad \text{SE}=\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}$$

Holds for (almost) any population distribution with finite variance ÔÇö that's the point of the CLT. $n\ge30$ is the usual rule of thumb; if the population is already normal, $\bar X$ is exactly normal for any $n$.

Decision: reject $H_0$ if p-value $<\alpha$ (or |statistic| > critical value). Type I error = reject true $H_0$ (prob $=\alpha$); Type II = fail to reject false $H_0$ (prob $=\beta$); power $=1-\beta$. Independence test df $=(r-1)(c-1)$; GOF df $=k-1$. $t$ has heavier tails than $z$ and $t_{df}\to N(0,1)$ as $df\to\infty$.

Exam traps
  • Sample variance divides by $n-1$, not $n$ (unbiasedness).
  • Var$(X-Y)$ = Var$X$ + Var$Y$ for independent $X,Y$ ÔÇö variances add even under subtraction.
  • Two-tailed test at $\alpha=0.05$ uses $z=1.96$; one-tailed uses $1.645$.
  • Binomial "at least one" questions: $P(\ge1)=1-P(0)$.
