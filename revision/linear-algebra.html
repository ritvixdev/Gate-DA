­ƒöÁ Linear Algebra

Every formula and exam-critical fact, condensed for last-week revision.

1 ┬À Vectors, norms & distance
$$x^{\mathsf T}y=\sum_i x_i y_i \qquad \|x\|_2=\sqrt{x^{\mathsf T}x} \qquad \|x\|_1=\sum_i|x_i| \qquad \|x\|_\infty=\max_i|x_i|$$
$$d(x,y)=\|x-y\|_2 \qquad \cos\theta=\frac{x^{\mathsf T}y}{\|x\|\,\|y\|} \qquad x\perp y \iff x^{\mathsf T}y=0$$

CauchyÔÇôSchwarz: $|x^{\mathsf T}y|\le\|x\|\|y\|$. Triangle: $\|x+y\|\le\|x\|+\|y\|$.

Linear independence: $\sum c_i v_i=0 \Rightarrow$ all $c_i=0$. Any $n+1$ vectors in $\mathbb{R}^n$ are dependent.

2 ┬À Matrices & matrixÔÇôvector product

$Ax$ = linear combination of columns of $A$ with weights $x$. $(AB)_{ij}=\sum_k a_{ik}b_{kj}$; defined when cols($A$) = rows($B$). $AB\ne BA$ in general.

$$(AB)^{\mathsf T}=B^{\mathsf T}A^{\mathsf T} \qquad (AB)^{-1}=B^{-1}A^{-1} \qquad (A^{\mathsf T})^{-1}=(A^{-1})^{\mathsf T}$$

Special matrices: symmetric $A^{\mathsf T}=A$; orthogonal $Q^{\mathsf T}Q=I$ (so $Q^{-1}=Q^{\mathsf T}$, $\|Qx\|=\|x\|$, $\det Q=\pm1$); idempotent/projection $P^2=P$ (eigenvalues 0 or 1); trace $\operatorname{tr}(A)=\sum a_{ii}=\sum\lambda_i$, $\operatorname{tr}(AB)=\operatorname{tr}(BA)$.

$$\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix}^{-1}=\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{pmatrix}d&-b\\-c&a\end{pmatrix}$$
3 ┬À Determinants
$$\det(AB)=\det A\,\det B \qquad \det(A^{\mathsf T})=\det A \qquad \det(A^{-1})=\tfrac1{\det A} \qquad \det(cA)=c^n\det A$$

Triangular/diagonal matrix: $\det=$ product of diagonal entries. Row swap flips sign; row $\times c$ scales $\det$ by $c$; adding a multiple of one row to another leaves $\det$ unchanged. $\det A=\prod_i\lambda_i$.

$A$ invertible $\iff \det A\ne0 \iff$ rank $=n \iff$ columns independent $\iff Ax=0$ only at $x=0$ $\iff 0$ is not an eigenvalue.

4 ┬À Rank & null space
$$\operatorname{rank}(A)+\dim N(A)=n \quad (A\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times n},\ n=\text{\#columns})$$

rank = # pivots = # independent rows = # independent columns = # non-zero singular values. $\operatorname{rank}(A)\le\min(m,n)$; $\operatorname{rank}(AB)\le\min(\operatorname{rank}A,\operatorname{rank}B)$; $\operatorname{rank}(A^{\mathsf T}A)=\operatorname{rank}(A)$.

Null space $N(A)=\{x:Ax=0\}$; always contains $0$; non-trivial $\iff$ rank $< n$. Outer product $uv^{\mathsf T}$ has rank 1.

5 ┬À Solving $Ax=b$ & pseudo-inverse

Consistency (Rouch├®ÔÇôCapelli): solution exists $\iff \operatorname{rank}(A)=\operatorname{rank}([A|b])$. If $=n$: unique; if $<n$: infinitely many ($\infty^{\,n-r}$ family). No solution if $\operatorname{rank}([A|b])>\operatorname{rank}(A)$.

$$\textbf{Least squares: } \hat x=\arg\min\|Ax-b\|^2 \;\Rightarrow\; A^{\mathsf T}A\hat x=A^{\mathsf T}b \;\Rightarrow\; \hat x=(A^{\mathsf T}A)^{-1}A^{\mathsf T}b$$
$$\textbf{Pseudo-inverse: } A^{+}=(A^{\mathsf T}A)^{-1}A^{\mathsf T}\ (\text{full col rank}) \qquad A^{+}=V\Sigma^{+}U^{\mathsf T}\ (\text{via SVD, } \Sigma^+ \text{ inverts non-zero } \sigma_i)$$

$\hat x=A^{+}b$ gives the least-squares solution; among all minimizers it has minimum norm. If $A$ invertible, $A^{+}=A^{-1}$.

6 ┬À Eigenvalues & eigenvectors
$$Av=\lambda v\ (v\ne0) \qquad \det(A-\lambda I)=0 \qquad \sum\lambda_i=\operatorname{tr}A \qquad \prod\lambda_i=\det A$$

For $2\times2$: $\lambda^2-\operatorname{tr}(A)\lambda+\det(A)=0$. Eigenvalues of $A^k$ are $\lambda^k$; of $A^{-1}$ are $1/\lambda$; of $A+cI$ are $\lambda+c$. Triangular matrix: eigenvalues = diagonal. $A$ and $A^{\mathsf T}$ share eigenvalues.

Real symmetric $A$: all eigenvalues real; eigenvectors of distinct eigenvalues orthogonal; always diagonalizable ($A=Q\Lambda Q^{\mathsf T}$, spectral theorem). PSD $\iff$ all $\lambda_i\ge0$; PD $\iff$ all $\lambda_i>0$.

7 ┬À Diagonalization
$$A=PDP^{-1} \qquad A^k=PD^kP^{-1}$$

Possible $\iff$ $A$ has $n$ independent eigenvectors ($n$ distinct eigenvalues is sufficient, not necessary). Geometric multiplicity $\le$ algebraic multiplicity; equality for every $\lambda$ $\iff$ diagonalizable.

8 ┬À Projections & orthogonality
$$\textbf{Onto vector } a:\ \operatorname{proj}_a b=\frac{a^{\mathsf T}b}{a^{\mathsf T}a}\,a \qquad \textbf{Onto } C(A):\ P=A(A^{\mathsf T}A)^{-1}A^{\mathsf T}$$

$P^2=P$, $P^{\mathsf T}=P$; eigenvalues 0/1; $\operatorname{tr}(P)=\operatorname{rank}(P)=\dim$ of subspace. Residual $b-Pb\perp C(A)$. GramÔÇôSchmidt: $u_k=v_k-\sum_{j<k}\operatorname{proj}_{u_j}v_k$, then normalize.

Centering matrix $M=I-\tfrac1n\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^{\mathsf T}$: symmetric projection, eigenvalues $1$ ($n{-}1$ times) and $0$, trace $n-1$. A recurring exam favorite.

9 ┬À SVD & PCA
$$A=U\Sigma V^{\mathsf T},\quad \sigma_i=\sqrt{\lambda_i(A^{\mathsf T}A)}\ \ge 0$$

$V$: eigenvectors of $A^{\mathsf T}A$; $U$: eigenvectors of $AA^{\mathsf T}$. # non-zero $\sigma_i$ = rank. $\|A\|_2=\sigma_{\max}$. Exists for any matrix (unlike eigendecomposition).

PCA: center data ÔåÆ covariance $C=\tfrac1{n-1}X^{\mathsf T}X$ ÔåÆ PCs = eigenvectors of $C$ sorted by eigenvalue; $\lambda_i$ = variance along PC$_i$; explained variance ratio $=\lambda_i/\sum\lambda_j$. Distinct PCs are orthogonal ($90^\circ$).

Exam traps
  • $\det(A+B)\ne\det A+\det B$ and $\operatorname{rank}(A+B)\le\operatorname{rank}A+\operatorname{rank}B$ (not equality).
  • Symmetric $\ne$ invertible: the centering matrix is symmetric, idempotent, but singular.
  • Rotation matrix by $\theta$: $M^k$ = rotation by $k\theta$; $M^n=I$ when $n\theta$ is a multiple of $2\pi$.
  • For NAT rank questions, row-reduce ÔÇö don't guess from matrix size.
