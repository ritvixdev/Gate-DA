Q1. Events $A$ and $B$ are independent with $P(A)=0.5$ and $P(B)=0.4$. Then $P(A\cup B)$ equals
Single correct (MCQ).
$P(A\cap B)=0.5\times0.4=0.2$ (independence), so $P(A\cup B)=0.5+0.4-0.2=0.7$.

Q2. The rank of $\begin{pmatrix}1&2&3\\2&4&6\\1&1&1\end{pmatrix}$ is
Single correct (MCQ).
Row 2 $=2\times$ Row 1, so it adds nothing. Rows 1 and 3 are independent → rank $=2$.

Q4. The minimum value of $f(x)=x^2-4x+7$ over $\mathbb{R}$ is
Single correct (MCQ).
$f'(x)=2x-4=0\Rightarrow x=2$; $f''=2>0$ so it's a minimum. $f(2)=4-8+7=3$.

Q11. The largest eigenvalue of $\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix}$ is ______.
Numerical answer (NAT).
$\lambda^2-\operatorname{tr}\lambda+\det=\lambda^2-4\lambda+3=0\Rightarrow\lambda=1,3$. Largest $=3$. (Shortcut for $\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\b&a\end{pmatrix}$: $\lambda=a\pm b$.)

Q12. A disease affects 1% of a population. A test detects it with sensitivity 99% and has a false-positive rate of 5%. The probability that a person who tests positive actually has the disease is
Single correct (MCQ).
Bayes: $\dfrac{0.99\times0.01}{0.99\times0.01+0.05\times0.99}=\dfrac{0.0099}{0.0099+0.0495}=\dfrac{0.0099}{0.0594}=\dfrac16\approx0.167$.
