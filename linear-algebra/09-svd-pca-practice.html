SVD & PCA

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. The singular values of $A$ are the…
Only for symmetric PSD matrices.
✅ Correct — $\sigma_i=\sqrt{\lambda_i(A^{\mathsf T}A)}$, always real and $\ge0$.
Unrelated in general.
That relates to $A^{-1}$, not singular values.
Q2. In $A=U\Sigma V^{\mathsf T}$, $U$ and $V$ are…
$\Sigma$ is diagonal; $U,V$ are not.
They're invertible (orthogonal), not singular.
✅ Correct — $U$ and $V$ are orthogonal; $\Sigma$ is the diagonal of singular values.
They're orthogonal, not triangular.
Q3. Can a singular value be negative?
No — they're square roots of non-negative numbers.
✅ Correct — singular values are always $\ge0$ by definition.
Even if $A$ has negative entries, $\sigma_i\ge0$.
Dimension is irrelevant — they're never negative.
Q4. $A^{\mathsf T}A$ has eigenvalues $16,9$. Singular values of $A$?
Those are the eigenvalues; take square roots.
✅ Correct — $\sigma_i=\sqrt{\lambda_i}=\sqrt{16},\sqrt{9}=4,3$.
That squares them; you need the square root.
Halving isn't the same as square-rooting.
Q5. A matrix has singular values $5,2,0,0$. Its rank?
Only the non-zero singular values count.
✅ Correct — rank = number of non-zero singular values $=2$.
Two singular values are non-zero, so rank $>0$.
That's their sum, not the rank.
Q6. Which matrices have an SVD?
SVD works for rectangular matrices too.
No symmetry needed.
✅ Correct — every $m\times n$ matrix has an SVD (unlike diagonalization).
Even rank-deficient matrices have one.
Q7. PCA is essentially…
Unrelated — that solves systems.
✅ Correct — PCA = SVD of the centered data; components are eigenvectors of the covariance.
PCA doesn't invert anything.
Cramer's rule solves small systems, not PCA.
Q8. The best rank-$k$ approximation of $A$ keeps the…
Keeping the smallest loses the most information.
It's about singular values, not raw columns.
✅ Correct — keep the top-$k$ $\sigma_i u_i v_i^{\mathsf T}$ terms (strongest directions).
Singular values are never negative.
