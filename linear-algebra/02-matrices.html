Matrices

A matrix is a rectangular array of numbers — and, more powerfully, a linear transformation of space. Master the operations, types, and the geometry.

🔢 Matrix basics

An $m \times n$ matrix has $m$ rows and $n$ columns; entry $a_{ij}$ sits in row $i$, column $j$.

$$ A = [a_{ij}],\quad i=1\dots m,\ j=1\dots n \qquad \text{square: } m=n,\quad \text{column vector: } n\times 1 $$
✖️ Matrix multiplication

If $A$ is $m\times n$ and $B$ is $n\times p$, then $C=AB$ is $m\times p$. Each entry is a dot product of a row of $A$ with a column of $B$:

$$ C_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} A_{ik} B_{kj} $$
✦ Worked example

$A=\begin{bmatrix}1&2\\3&4\end{bmatrix},\ B=\begin{bmatrix}5&6\\7&8\end{bmatrix}$

$C_{11}=1\cdot5+2\cdot7=19,\quad C_{12}=1\cdot6+2\cdot8=22$

$\Rightarrow AB=\begin{bmatrix}19&22\\43&50\end{bmatrix}$

⚡ Critical

$AB \neq BA$ in general — matrix multiplication is not commutative. A classic GATE trap.

🔄 Transpose & inverse
$$ (A^{\mathsf T})_{ij}=A_{ji},\qquad (AB)^{\mathsf T}=B^{\mathsf T}A^{\mathsf T} $$ $$ AA^{-1}=A^{-1}A=I,\qquad (AB)^{-1}=B^{-1}A^{-1},\qquad A^{-1}\text{ exists} \iff \det A \neq 0 $$ $$ \begin{bmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{bmatrix}^{-1}=\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix}d&-b\\-c&a\end{bmatrix} $$
🧠 Memory trick

“Socks before shoes, shoes before socks to remove.” For an inverse or transpose of a product, the order reverses.

⚡ Trace
$$ \operatorname{tr}(A)=\sum_i a_{ii},\quad \operatorname{tr}(AB)=\operatorname{tr}(BA),\quad \operatorname{tr}(A)=\textstyle\sum \lambda_i $$

The trace equals the sum of the eigenvalues — a fast sanity check in exams.

Q1. If $A$ is $3\times3$ with $A^2=A$, which must be true?
$A=I$ works, but so do many other matrices — not forced.
$A=0$ works but is not the only case.
✅ Correct — $A^2=A \Rightarrow \lambda^2=\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda\in\{0,1\}$.
$\det$ could be $0$ (if $A=0$) or $1$ (if $A=I$).
