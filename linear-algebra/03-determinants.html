Determinants

A single number that tells you whether a matrix is invertible, how much it scales area or volume, and whether it flips orientation.

⬛ What it means

For a square matrix $A$, the determinant $\det(A)$ (or $|A|$) encodes:

  • $\det(A)=0 \Rightarrow A$ is singular (not invertible).
  • $|\det(A)|$ is the area / volume scaling factor of the transformation.
  • $\operatorname{sign}(\det)$ is orientation: $+$ preserves, $-$ flips.

🧮 Computing determinants
$$ \det\begin{bmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{bmatrix}=ad-bc $$ $$ \det\begin{bmatrix}a&b&c\\d&e&f\\g&h&i\end{bmatrix}=a(ei-fh)-b(di-fg)+c(dh-eg) $$

The cofactor signs follow a checkerboard $\begin{smallmatrix}+&-&+\\-&+&-\\+&-&+\end{smallmatrix}$.

✦ Worked example

$\det\begin{bmatrix}2&1&3\\0&4&2\\1&0&5\end{bmatrix}=2(20-0)-1(0-2)+3(0-4)=40+2-12=30$

⚙️ Key properties
$$ \det(AB)=\det A\,\det B,\qquad \det(A^{\mathsf T})=\det A,\qquad \det(A^{-1})=\tfrac{1}{\det A} $$ $$ \det(cA)=c^{n}\det A,\qquad \det(A)=\textstyle\prod \lambda_i $$
  • Swap two rows → sign flips.
  • Multiply a row by $k$ → determinant $\times k$.
  • Add a multiple of one row to another → unchanged.
  • Triangular / diagonal → product of the diagonal entries.
⚡ GATE shortcut

For triangular or diagonal matrices, $\det=$ product of the diagonal. Huge time-saver.

🔗 Cramer's rule

For $Ax=b$ with $\det A \neq 0$:

$$ x_i = \frac{\det(A_i)}{\det(A)} \quad\text{where } A_i = A \text{ with column } i \text{ replaced by } b $$
⚡ When to use

Cramer's rule is mostly theoretical in GATE. For actual solving, Gaussian elimination is faster.

Practice

Q1. If $\det(A)=5$ for a $3\times3$ matrix, what is $\det(2A)$?
$\det(cA)=c^n\det A$, so $2^3\cdot5=40$, not 10.

✅ Correct — $\det(2A)=2^3\cdot5=40$.
