System of Linear Equations

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. $r=\operatorname{rank}(A)=2$, $r'=\operatorname{rank}([A|b])=3$, $n=3$. Solutions?
✅ Correct — $r\neq r'$ ⇒ inconsistent ⇒ no solution.
Unique needs $r=r'=n$.
Infinite needs $r=r'<n$.
Linear systems never have exactly two solutions.
Q2. $r=r'=2$, $n=4$. Solutions?
Consistent since $r=r'$.
Unique needs $r=n$; here $r=2<4$.
✅ Correct — $r=r'<n$ ⇒ infinite, with $n-r=2$ free variables.
Free vars $=n-r=2$, not $4$.
Q3. $Ax=0$ ($A$ square) has a non-trivial solution iff…
That gives only the trivial solution $x=0$.
✅ Correct — $\det A=0 \iff \operatorname{rank}<n \iff$ non-trivial solutions exist.
Homogeneous means $b=0$ by definition.
It happens exactly when $A$ is singular.
Q4. A homogeneous system $Ax=0$ is…
✅ Correct — $x=0$ always works, so it's never inconsistent.
It can never be inconsistent — $x=0$ is always a solution.
It may also have infinitely many (if singular).
It's always solvable (at least trivially).
Q5. Square $A$ gives a unique solution for every $b$ iff…
$\det=0$ means no unique solution.
Symmetry isn't the condition.
✅ Correct — $\det A\neq0$ ⇒ $A^{-1}$ exists ⇒ $x=A^{-1}b$ unique.
That's only the homogeneous case.
Q6. The general solution of $Ax=b$ has the form…
That solves $Ax=0$, not $Ax=b$.
✅ Correct — particular solution $+$ any null-space vector: $x=x_p+x_h$.
Misses the infinitely many shifts by null-space vectors.
Solutions add, they don't multiply.
Q7. For what $k$ does $x+2y=3,\ 2x+ky=7$ have no solution?
$\det=k-4=-1\neq0$ ⇒ unique solution.
✅ Correct — $\det=k-4=0$ at $k=4$; then $R_2-2R_1$ gives $[0\,0\,|\,1]$, so $r'\neq r$ → no solution.
$\det\neq0$ ⇒ unique.
$\det=-2\neq0$ ⇒ unique.
Q8. Cramer's rule applies when…
It divides by $\det A$, so $\det A=0$ breaks it.
It needs a square coefficient matrix.
✅ Correct — $x_i=\det(A_i)/\det(A)$, valid for square $A$ with $\det A\neq0$.
Only for invertible square systems.
