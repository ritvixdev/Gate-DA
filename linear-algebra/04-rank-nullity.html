Rank & Nullity

Rank measures how much information a matrix carries; nullity measures what it destroys. Together they always sum to the number of columns.

📐 Rank of a matrix

The rank of $A$ is the maximum number of linearly independent rows (equivalently, columns). It equals the number of non-zero rows in row-echelon form.

$$ \operatorname{rank}(A)=\operatorname{rank}(A^{\mathsf T}),\qquad \operatorname{rank}(A)\le \min(m,n) $$ $$ \operatorname{rank}(AB)\le \min(\operatorname{rank}A,\operatorname{rank}B) $$
✦ Worked example

$A=\begin{bmatrix}1&2&3\\2&4&6\\1&1&1\end{bmatrix}$. Row 2 $=2\cdot$Row 1, so it vanishes. Row-reduction leaves 2 non-zero rows $\Rightarrow \operatorname{rank}(A)=2$.

🕳️ Null space & nullity

The null space (kernel) of $A$ is every $x$ with $Ax=0$.

$$ \operatorname{Null}(A)=\{x\in\mathbb{R}^n : Ax=0\},\qquad \operatorname{nullity}(A)=\dim\operatorname{Null}(A)=n-\operatorname{rank}(A) $$
🧠 The rank-nullity theorem

For an $m\times n$ matrix:

$\operatorname{rank}(A) + \operatorname{nullity}(A) = n$

One of the most important identities in all of linear algebra.

🗄️ Four fundamental subspaces
SubspaceDefinitionDimension
Column space $C(A)$span of columns$\operatorname{rank}(A)$
Null space $N(A)$$\{x:Ax=0\}$$n-\operatorname{rank}(A)$
Row space $C(A^{\mathsf T})$span of rows$\operatorname{rank}(A)$
Left null space $N(A^{\mathsf T})$$\{y:A^{\mathsf T}y=0\}$$m-\operatorname{rank}(A)$
⚡ GATE insight

Row space $\perp$ null space in $\mathbb{R}^n$; column space $\perp$ left null space in $\mathbb{R}^m$. Handy for system-of-equations questions.

Practice

Q1. A $5\times8$ matrix $A$ has rank $3$. What is its nullity?
Nullity $=n-\operatorname{rank}=8-3=5$; $n$ is the column count (8).
Don't confuse rank with nullity. Nullity $=8-3=5$.
✅ Correct — nullity $=n-\operatorname{rank}=8-3=5$.
8 is the number of columns, not the nullity.

GATE takeaway — rank + nullity = number of columns; this is the identity most rank questions hinge on.
