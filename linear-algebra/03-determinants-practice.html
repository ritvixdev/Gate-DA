Determinants

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. $\det(A)=5$ for a $3\times3$ matrix. $\det(2A)=?$
That used $2\times$; the rule is $c^n$, not $c$.
That used $2^2$; for a $3\times3$ the exponent is $3$.
✅ Correct — $\det(cA)=c^n\det A=2^3\cdot5=40$.
Scaling the matrix scales the determinant by $c^n$.
Q2. $\det\begin{bmatrix}2&7&1\\0&3&8\\0&0&5\end{bmatrix}=?$
✅ Correct — upper-triangular → product of diagonal $=2\cdot3\cdot5=30$.
Triangular det is the product, not a partial one.
No zero on the diagonal, so the product isn't $0$.
$17$ is the diagonal sum (trace), not the determinant.
Q3. Which is true?
False — det is multiplicative, not additive.
False — that's the trace; det is the product.
✅ Correct — determinant is multiplicative over products.
False — $\det(A^{\mathsf T})=\det A$ (no sign change).
Q4. $\det A=4,\ \det B=3$. $\det(AB)=?$
That's the sum; det multiplies.
✅ Correct — $\det(AB)=\det A\,\det B=4\cdot3=12$.
That would need $\det B=1/\det A$.
$4/3=\det(AB^{-1})$, not $\det(AB)$.
Q5. $\det A=5$. $\det(A^{-1})=?$
Inverse flips the determinant to its reciprocal.
✅ Correct — $\det(A^{-1})=1/\det A=1/5$.
Sign doesn't flip; it's the reciprocal.
$25=(\det A)^2=\det(A^2)$.
Q6. Eigenvalues of $A$ are $2,-1,4$. $\det A=?$
$5$ is the trace (sum), not the determinant.
✅ Correct — $\det=\prod\lambda=2\cdot(-1)\cdot4=-8$ (negative ⇒ orientation flips).
Watch the sign — one eigenvalue is $-1$.
No eigenvalue is $0$, so the product isn't $0$.
Q7. $\det\begin{bmatrix}1&2\\2&4\end{bmatrix}=?$ — and what does it mean?
✅ Correct — $1\cdot4-2\cdot2=0$; column 2 $=2\times$ column 1, so it's singular (collapses to a line).
$ad-bc=4-4=0$, not $8$.
It's $0$ — the rows are proportional.
Recompute: $4-4=0$.
Q8. If $A$ is orthogonal, $\det A$ can be…
No — orthogonality pins it down.
$0$ is impossible — orthogonal matrices are invertible.
✅ Correct — $\det(A^{\mathsf T}A)=\det I=1\Rightarrow(\det A)^2=1\Rightarrow\det A=\pm1$.
$-1$ is allowed too (a reflection).
