Linear Regression

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. The least-squares line always passes through…
Only if the means are at the origin.
No single data point is guaranteed.
✅ Correct — it always passes through the means.
Not the extreme point.
Q2. $\operatorname{Cov}(x,y)=6,\ \operatorname{Var}(x)=10$. Slope $\beta_1$?
You inverted the ratio.
✅ Correct — $\beta_1=\operatorname{Cov}/\operatorname{Var}=6/10=0.6$.
That's a product.
That's a sum.
Q3. $\bar{x}=3,\ \bar{y}=4,\ \beta_1=0.6$. Intercept?
That's $\bar{y}$; subtract $\beta_1\bar{x}$.
✅ Correct — $\beta_0=4-0.6(3)=2.2$.
You added instead of subtracted.
That's $\beta_1\bar{x}$, not the intercept.
Q4. In "linear" regression, linearity is in the…
You can use $x^2$ as a feature and still be linear regression.
The target is the output, not the condition.
✅ Correct — linear in the $\beta$'s.
Residuals are errors.
Q5. $R^2=1$ means…
That's $R^2=0$.
That's $R^2=0.5$.
✅ Correct — all variance explained; residuals are zero.
A perfect fit on test data isn't necessarily overfitting.
Q6. A residual is…
That's the prediction.
✅ Correct — the gap between actual and predicted.
Not a product.
The slope is $\beta_1$.
Q7. Predict $x=6$ for $\hat{y}=2.2+0.6x$.
That's just $0.6\cdot6$; add the intercept.
Recompute: $2.2+3.6=5.8$.
✅ Correct — $2.2+0.6(6)=5.8$.
Plug into the equation.
Q8. A great fit on data proves $x$ causes $y$?
Correlation isn't causation.
✅ Correct — a strong fit shows association, not causation.
Even a perfect fit doesn't prove causation.
Adding features doesn't establish causation.
