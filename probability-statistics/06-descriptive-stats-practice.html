Descriptive Statistics

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. Data 3, 5, 7. The mean is…
That's the min.
✅ Correct — $(3+5+7)/3=5$.
That's the max.
That's the sum, not the average.
Q2. Same data 3, 5, 7. Sample variance $s^2$?
That divides by $n=3$ (population style).
Recompute: $\sum(x-5)^2=8$, divide by $n-1=2$.
✅ Correct — $8/(3-1)=4$.
That's the sum of squared deviations, not divided.
Q3. Sample variance divides squared deviations by…
That's the biased (population) version.
Not a standard divisor.
✅ Correct — $n-1$ (Bessel's correction).
That appears in the standard error, not the variance.
Q4. Which summary resists outliers best?
The mean gets pulled by outliers.
✅ Correct — the median (and IQR) are robust.
Std is sensitive to outliers.
Range is the most outlier-sensitive of all.
Q5. IQR equals…
That's the range.
✅ Correct — IQR $=Q_3-Q_1$ (middle 50%).
$Q_2$ is the median.
That measures skew, not spread.
Q6. For a right-skewed dataset…
That's left-skew.
That's symmetric.
✅ Correct — the long right tail pulls the mean above the median.
Every dataset has a median.
Q7. A histogram is used for…
Categories use a bar chart.
✅ Correct — histograms bin numeric data to show its shape.
That's a scatter plot.
Binning hides exact values.
Q8. A boxplot displays…
It shows the median, not the mean.
That's a different plot.
✅ Correct — min, Q1, median, Q3, max + outliers.
Correlation needs two variables.
