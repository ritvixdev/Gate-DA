GATE DA 2025 Conditional expectation MCQ 1 mark
Q11. Suppose $X$ and $Y$ are random variables, with $E[X\mid Y]$ the conditional expectation of $X$ given $Y$. Then $E\big[E[X\mid Y]\big]$ equals
Single correct (MCQ).
By the tower property (law of total expectation), $E\big[E[X\mid Y]\big]=E[X]$.
GATE DA 2025 CDF MCQ 1 mark
Q19. A continuous random variable $X$ has CDF $F_X(x)=0$ for $x\le t$, $\dfrac{x-t}{4-t}$ for $t\le x\le4$, and $1$ for $x\ge4$. If the median of $X$ is $3$, what is $t$?
Single correct (MCQ).
Median: $F_X(3)=\tfrac12\Rightarrow\dfrac{3-t}{4-t}=\tfrac12\Rightarrow t=2$.
GATE DA 2025 Normal distribution MCQ 1 mark
Q20. Let $X=aZ+b$ with $Z$ standard normal and constants $a,b$. Given $E[X]=1$, $E\big[(X-E[X])Z\big]=-2$, and $E\big[(X-E[X])^2\big]=4$, the values of $a,b$ are:
Single correct (MCQ).
$E[X]=b=1$. Since $X-E[X]=aZ$, $E[(aZ)Z]=a=-2$ (and $a^2=4$ is consistent). So $a=-2,\ b=1$.
GATE DA 2025 Exponential distribution MCQ 1 mark
Q21. For an exponentially distributed $X$ with $E[X]=\tfrac1\lambda$, it is given that $P(X\ge2)=0.25$. What is $\lambda$? ($\ln$ is natural log.)
Single correct (MCQ).
$P(X\ge2)=e^{-2\lambda}=0.25\Rightarrow -2\lambda=\ln\tfrac14\Rightarrow\lambda=\tfrac{\ln4}{2}=\ln2$.
GATE DA 2025 Bayes' theorem NAT 1 mark
Q31. Box-1 has 2 black & 1 white, Box-2 has 1 black & 2 white, Box-3 has 3 black & 3 white. A box is chosen with $P(\text{Box-1})=\tfrac12$, $P(\text{Box-2})=\tfrac16$, $P(\text{Box-3})=\tfrac13$, then a ball is drawn. Given it is white, the probability it came from Box-2 is ______ (2 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
$P(\text{white})=\tfrac12\!\cdot\!\tfrac13+\tfrac16\!\cdot\!\tfrac23+\tfrac13\!\cdot\!\tfrac12=\tfrac49$. So $P(\text{Box-2}\mid\text{white})=\dfrac{\tfrac16\cdot\tfrac23}{\tfrac49}=0.25$.
GATE DA 2025 Chi-square MCQ 2 marks
Q36. Let $Y=Z^2$ where $Z=\dfrac{X-\mu}{\sigma}$ and $X$ is normal with mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$. The variance of $Y$ is
Single correct (MCQ).
$Z$ is standard normal, so $Y=Z^2\sim\chi^2_1$, which has variance $2$.
GATE DA 2025 CDF MCQ 2 marks
Q39. A random variable $X$ has CDF $F_X(x)=0$ for $x\le-1$, $\tfrac14(x+1)^2$ for $-1\le x\le1$, and $1$ for $x\ge1$. Find $P(X^2\le0.25)$.
Single correct (MCQ).
$P(X^2\le0.25)=P(-0.5\le X\le0.5)=F(0.5)-F(-0.5)=\tfrac14(1.5)^2-\tfrac14(0.5)^2=0.5$.
GATE DA 2025 Central Limit Theorem MCQ 2 marks
Q40. Let $Y=\sum_{i=1}^{300}X_i$ with $X_i\sim\text{Bernoulli}(0.25)$ i.i.d. Using the CLT ($\Phi$ = standard normal CDF), $P(60\le Y\le90)\approx$
Single correct (MCQ).
Mean $=300(0.25)=75$, variance $=300(0.25)(0.75)=56.25$, SD $=7.5$. $z=\pm\tfrac{15}{7.5}=\pm2$, giving $\Phi(2)-\Phi(-2)$.
GATE DA 2025 Random variables MCQ 2 marks
Q41. Let $Y=\lfloor X\rfloor$ where $X$ is exponential with mean $\tfrac{1}{\ln 10}$. For a positive integer $\ell$, $P(Y=\ell)=q^{\ell}(1-q)$. Find $q$.
Single correct (MCQ).
$\lambda=\ln 10$, so $P(Y=\ell)=e^{-\lambda\ell}(1-e^{-\lambda})=(0.1)^{\ell}(0.9)$. Hence $q=0.1$.
GATE DA 2025 Probability MCQ 2 marks
Q45. $100$ fair dice (faces $1$–$6$) are thrown. Event $A$: at least one die shows a $1$. Then $P(A)=$
Single correct (MCQ).
$P(\text{no die shows }1)=\left(\tfrac56\right)^{100}$, so $P(A)=1-\left(\tfrac56\right)^{100}$.
GATE DA 2025 Sample statistics MSQ 2 marks
Q54. A coin shows heads with probability $p$. With $X_i=1$ for head (else $0$) over $n$ independent tosses, let $\hat p=\tfrac1n\sum_{i=1}^n X_i$. Which is/are correct?
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
$E[\hat p]=p$ (A). $\operatorname{Var}(\hat p)=\tfrac{p(1-p)}{n}$, which decreases with $n$ (C). B and D are false.
GATE DA 2025 Expectation NAT 2 marks
Q61. A bag has $5$ white and $10$ black balls. $n$ balls are drawn one at a time with replacement; $S_n$ is the number of black balls drawn. Find $E[S_{100}]$ (1 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
Each draw is black with probability $\tfrac{10}{15}=\tfrac23$, so $E[S_{100}]=100\cdot\tfrac23\approx66.7$.
GATE DA 2024 Distributions MCQ 1 mark
Q11. Consider the statements: (i) the mean and variance of a Poisson random variable are equal; (ii) for a standard normal random variable, the mean is zero and the variance is one. Which ONE is correct?
Single correct (MCQ).
Poisson$(\lambda)$ has mean $=$ variance $=\lambda$; the standard normal is $\mathcal{N}(0,1)$. Both statements hold.
GATE DA 2024 Probability MCQ 1 mark
Q12. Three fair coins are tossed independently. $T$ is the event of two or more heads; $S$ is the event of two or more tails. What is $P(T\cap S)$?
Single correct (MCQ).
With only three tosses you cannot get $\ge2$ heads and $\ge2$ tails at once, so $T\cap S=\varnothing$ and $P=0$.
GATE DA 2024 Conditional independence MCQ 1 mark
Q24. Five random variables have joint distribution $P(V,W,X,Y,Z)=P(V)\,P(W)\,P(X\mid V,W)\,P(Y\mid X)\,P(Z\mid X)$. Which ONE statement is FALSE?
Single correct (MCQ).
$V$ and $W$ are parents of the common child $X$ (a collider); conditioning on $X$ makes them dependent, so C is false. $X$ separates the others, so A, B, D are true.
GATE DA 2024 Descriptive statistics MCQ 1 mark
Q27. For the attribute income, the min, max, mean and standard deviation are $46000$, $170000$, $96000$ and $21000$ (in $\textsf{₹}$). The z-score normalized value of $\textsf{₹}106000$ is closest to:
Single correct (MCQ).
$z=\dfrac{x-\mu}{\sigma}=\dfrac{106000-96000}{21000}\approx0.476$ (the min/max are distractors).
GATE DA 2024 Sample statistics NAT 1 mark
Q34. The sample average of 50 data points is 40. The updated sample average after including a new data point of value 142 is ______.
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
$\dfrac{50\times40+142}{51}=\dfrac{2142}{51}=42$.
GATE DA 2024 Expectation MCQ 2 marks
Q36. A fair six-sided die is thrown repeatedly and independently. What is the expected number of throws until two consecutive even numbers appear?
Single correct (MCQ).
With $p=P(\text{even})=\tfrac12$, the expected wait for two consecutive successes is $\dfrac1p+\dfrac1{p^2}=2+4=6$.
GATE DA 2024 Random variables NAT 2 marks
Q56. Let $X\sim\text{Uniform}[1,3]$ and $Y\sim\text{Uniform}[2,4]$ be independent. The probability $P(X\ge Y)$ is ______ (3 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
With joint density $\tfrac14$ on $[1,3]\times[2,4]$, the region $x\ge y$ has area $\int_2^3 (x-2)\,dx=\tfrac12$, so $P=\tfrac14\cdot\tfrac12=0.125$.
GATE DA 2024 Distributions NAT 2 marks
Q57. Let $X\sim\text{Exponential}(\lambda)$ with pdf $f(x)=\lambda e^{-\lambda x}$ for $x\ge0$. If $5\,E(X)=\operatorname{Var}(X)$, the value of $\lambda$ is ______ (1 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
$E(X)=\tfrac1\lambda$, $\operatorname{Var}(X)=\tfrac1{\lambda^2}$. So $\tfrac5\lambda=\tfrac1{\lambda^2}\Rightarrow\lambda=0.2$.
GATE DA 2024 Conditional probability NAT 2 marks
Q58. For events $T$ and $S$: $P(T^c)=0.6$, $P(S\mid T)=0.3$, $P(S\mid T^c)=0.6$. Then $P(T\mid S)$ is ______ (2 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
$P(S)=0.3(0.4)+0.6(0.6)=0.48$, so $P(T\mid S)=\dfrac{0.3\times0.4}{0.48}=0.25$.

GATE DA 2024 Covariance NAT 2 marks
Q65. Two fair coins are tossed independently. $X=1$ if both tosses are heads (else $0$); $Y=1$ if at least one toss is heads (else $0$). The covariance of $X$ and $Y$ is ______ (3 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
$E[XY]=P(\text{both heads})=\tfrac14$, $E[X]=\tfrac14$, $E[Y]=\tfrac34$. $\operatorname{Cov}=\tfrac14-\tfrac14\cdot\tfrac34=\tfrac1{16}=0.0625$.
