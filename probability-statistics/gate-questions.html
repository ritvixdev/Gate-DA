GATE DA 2024 Distributions
Q1. Consider the statements: (i) the mean and variance of a Poisson random variable are equal; (ii) for a standard normal random variable, the mean is zero and the variance is one. Which ONE is correct?
✓ Correct — a Poisson$(\lambda)$ has mean $=$ variance $=\lambda$, and the standard normal is $\mathcal{N}(0,1)$ by definition.
(ii) is also true: the standard normal has mean $0$, variance $1$.
(i) is also true: Poisson mean $=$ variance $=\lambda$.
Both statements are in fact true.
GATE DA 2024 Conditional probability
Q2. Three fair coins are tossed independently. $T$ is the event that two or more tosses are heads; $S$ is the event that two or more tosses are tails. What is $P(T\cap S)$?
✓ Correct — with only $3$ tosses you cannot have $\ge2$ heads and $\ge2$ tails at once (that needs $\ge4$). So $T\cap S=\varnothing$ and $P=0$.
$T$ and $S$ cannot both happen with three coins.
The events are mutually exclusive here, so the intersection has probability $0$.
They can never co-occur with three tosses.
GATE DA 2024 Conditional independence
Q3. Five random variables have the joint distribution $P(V,W,X,Y,Z)=P(V)\,P(W)\,P(X\mid V,W)\,P(Y\mid X)\,P(Z\mid X)$. Which ONE of the following statements is FALSE?
True — $X$ separates $Y$ from $V$, so given $X$ they are independent.
True — $X$ separates $Z$ from $W$.
✓ This is the FALSE one — $V$ and $W$ are parents of the common child $X$ (a collider). Conditioning on $X$ makes them dependent, not independent.
True — $Y$ and $Z$ share the single parent $X$, so they are independent given $X$.
GATE DA 2024 Descriptive statistics
Q4. For the attribute income, the min, max, mean and standard deviation are $\textsf{₹}46000$, $\textsf{₹}170000$, $\textsf{₹}96000$ and $\textsf{₹}21000$. The z-score normalized value of $\textsf{₹}106000$ is closest to:
That would use the range; z-score uses the mean and standard deviation.
✓ Correct — $z=\dfrac{x-\mu}{\sigma}=\dfrac{106000-96000}{21000}=\dfrac{10000}{21000}\approx0.476$.
Recompute: $(106000-96000)/21000\approx0.476$.
That divides by a much smaller spread; the SD is $21000$.
GATE DA 2024 Expectation
Q5. A fair six-sided die is thrown repeatedly and independently. What is the expected number of throws until two consecutive even numbers appear?
That is the expected wait for a single even ($1/p=2$), not two in a row.
Close, but the run-of-two formula gives more.
✓ Correct — with $p=P(\text{even})=\tfrac12$, the expected wait for two consecutive successes is $\dfrac1p+\dfrac1{p^2}=2+4=6$.
Recompute: $\frac1p+\frac1{p^2}=2+4=6$.
