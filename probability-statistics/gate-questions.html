GATE DA 2026 Probability MCQ 1 mark
Q19. Let $M$ be a randomly chosen non-empty subset of $S=\{1,2,3,\dots,2026\}$. What is the probability that the product of all elements of $M$ is even?
Single correct (MCQ).
The product is odd only if $M$ contains no even number. With $1013$ odd numbers there are $2^{1013}-1$ non-empty all-odd subsets out of $2^{2026}-1$ total, so $P(\text{even})=1-\dfrac{2^{1013}-1}{2^{2026}-1}=\dfrac{2^{1013}(2^{1013}-1)}{2^{2026}-1}$.
GATE DA 2026 Counting probability MCQ 1 mark
Q20. A program returns a non-negative integer solution of $n_1+n_2+n_3+n_4=20$, chosen uniformly. What is the probability that all of $n_1,n_2,n_3,n_4$ are positive?
Single correct (MCQ).
Non-negative solutions: $\binom{23}{3}$ (stars and bars). For positive solutions set $m_i=n_i-1$, giving $m_1+\dots+m_4=16$ with $\binom{19}{3}$ solutions. So $P=\binom{19}{3}/\binom{23}{3}$.

GATE DA 2026 Exponential (memoryless) NAT 1 mark
Q34. Let $X$ be an exponentially distributed random variable with mean $\lambda>0$. If $P(X>5)=0.35$, then the conditional probability $P(X>10\mid X>5)$ is ______ (2 dp).

GATE DA 2026 Variance MCQ 2 marks
Q44. Let $X$ and $Y$ be independent, with $X\sim\text{Bernoulli}(p=0.3)$ and $Y\sim\text{Normal}(\mu=0,\ \sigma^2=100)$. What is the variance of $(2X-1)Y$?
Single correct (MCQ).
$2X-1\in\{-1,+1\}$, so $(2X-1)^2=1$ always. With $E[Y]=0$ and independence, $\operatorname{Var}\big((2X-1)Y\big)=E[(2X-1)^2]\,E[Y^2]=1\cdot100=100$.
GATE DA 2026 Chi-square distribution MSQ 2 marks
Q53. Let $X_1,\dots,X_n$ be independent $\text{Normal}(0,1)$ and $\bar X=\tfrac1n\sum_{i=1}^n X_i$. Which is/are correct?
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
A and B are standard results. $X_1^2+X_n^2\sim\chi^2_2$, which is exponential with mean $2$ (C). But $\sqrt n\,\bar X\sim\text{Normal}(0,1)$, so $(\sqrt n\,\bar X)^2\sim\chi^2_1$ — one df, not two (D false).
GATE DA 2026 CDF properties MSQ 2 marks
Q54. Let $X$ be a discrete random variable with CDF $F(x)$. Which is/are correct?
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
A CDF is non-decreasing (B) and, for a discrete variable, a step function with jumps (C). It can equal $0$ (so not always positive, A false) and is right-continuous, not left-continuous (D false).
GATE DA 2026 Bayes' theorem NAT 2 marks
Q57. A test for disease $D$: if a person has $D$ the test is positive $80\%$ of the time; if not, it is positive $10\%$ of the time. $D$ occurs in $30\%$ of the population. If a person tests positive, the probability they actually have $D$ is ______ (2 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
$P(D\mid +)=\dfrac{0.8\times0.3}{0.8\times0.3+0.1\times0.7}=\dfrac{0.24}{0.24+0.07}=\dfrac{0.24}{0.31}\approx0.77$.
GATE DA 2026 Variance identity NAT 2 marks
Q62. For a dataset $\{x_1,\dots,x_n\}$ with $n=100$, it is given that $\dfrac{1}{2000}\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=1}^{n}(x_i-x_j)^2=99$. With $\bar{x}=\tfrac1n\sum_i x_i$, find $\dfrac{1}{99}\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}(x_i-\bar{x})^2$.
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
Using $\sum_i\sum_j (x_i-x_j)^2=2n\sum_i(x_i-\bar{x})^2$: here $\sum_i\sum_j=2000\times99=198000$, so $\sum_i(x_i-\bar{x})^2=\dfrac{198000}{2\times100}=990$. Thus $\dfrac{990}{99}=10$.
GATE DA 2026 Correlation NAT 2 marks
Q63. Let $X\sim\text{Uniform}(-1,1)$, and let the conditional distribution of $Y$ given $X=x$ be $\text{Uniform}(x^2-0.1,\ x^2+0.1)$. The value of $\operatorname{correlation}(X,Y)$ is ______.
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
$E[Y\mid X]=X^2$, so $\operatorname{Cov}(X,Y)=E[X\cdot X^2]-E[X]E[Y]=E[X^3]-0=0$ (an odd moment of a symmetric variable). Hence the correlation is $0$.
GATE DA 2026 Probability NAT 2 marks
Q64. Let $A_{5\times5}$ have each entry $\sim\text{Bernoulli}(p=0.5)$ independently. The probability that the row-sum of the second row and the column-sum of the third column are both equal to $3$ is ______ (2 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
The entry at position $(2,3)$ is shared. If it is $1$, the other four in the row and the other four in the column must each sum to $2$ ($\binom42/2^4=\tfrac{6}{16}$ each); if it is $0$, each must sum to $3$ ($\binom43/2^4=\tfrac4{16}$). Total $=\tfrac12(\tfrac{6}{16})^2+\tfrac12(\tfrac4{16})^2\approx0.10$.
GATE DA 2025 Conditional expectation MCQ 1 mark
Q11. Suppose $X$ and $Y$ are random variables, with $E[X\mid Y]$ the conditional expectation of $X$ given $Y$. Then $E\big[E[X\mid Y]\big]$ equals
Single correct (MCQ).
By the tower property (law of total expectation), $E\big[E[X\mid Y]\big]=E[X]$.
GATE DA 2025 CDF MCQ 1 mark
Q19. A continuous random variable $X$ has CDF $F_X(x)=0$ for $x\le t$, $\dfrac{x-t}{4-t}$ for $t\le x\le4$, and $1$ for $x\ge4$. If the median of $X$ is $3$, what is $t$?
Single correct (MCQ).
Median: $F_X(3)=\tfrac12\Rightarrow\dfrac{3-t}{4-t}=\tfrac12\Rightarrow t=2$.
GATE DA 2025 Normal distribution MCQ 1 mark
Q20. Let $X=aZ+b$ with $Z$ standard normal and constants $a,b$. Given $E[X]=1$, $E\big[(X-E[X])Z\big]=-2$, and $E\big[(X-E[X])^2\big]=4$, the values of $a,b$ are:
Single correct (MCQ).
$E[X]=b=1$. Since $X-E[X]=aZ$, $E[(aZ)Z]=a=-2$ (and $a^2=4$ is consistent). So $a=-2,\ b=1$.
GATE DA 2025 Exponential distribution MCQ 1 mark
Q21. For an exponentially distributed $X$ with $E[X]=\tfrac1\lambda$, it is given that $P(X\ge2)=0.25$. What is $\lambda$? ($\ln$ is natural log.)
Single correct (MCQ).
$P(X\ge2)=e^{-2\lambda}=0.25\Rightarrow -2\lambda=\ln\tfrac14\Rightarrow\lambda=\tfrac{\ln4}{2}=\ln2$.
GATE DA 2025 Bayes' theorem NAT 1 mark
Q31. Box-1 has 2 black & 1 white, Box-2 has 1 black & 2 white, Box-3 has 3 black & 3 white. A box is chosen with $P(\text{Box-1})=\tfrac12$, $P(\text{Box-2})=\tfrac16$, $P(\text{Box-3})=\tfrac13$, then a ball is drawn. Given it is white, the probability it came from Box-2 is ______ (2 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
$P(\text{white})=\tfrac12\!\cdot\!\tfrac13+\tfrac16\!\cdot\!\tfrac23+\tfrac13\!\cdot\!\tfrac12=\tfrac49$. So $P(\text{Box-2}\mid\text{white})=\dfrac{\tfrac16\cdot\tfrac23}{\tfrac49}=0.25$.
GATE DA 2025 Chi-square MCQ 2 marks
Q36. Let $Y=Z^2$ where $Z=\dfrac{X-\mu}{\sigma}$ and $X$ is normal with mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$. The variance of $Y$ is
Single correct (MCQ).
$Z$ is standard normal, so $Y=Z^2\sim\chi^2_1$, which has variance $2$.
GATE DA 2025 CDF MCQ 2 marks
Q39. A random variable $X$ has CDF $F_X(x)=0$ for $x\le-1$, $\tfrac14(x+1)^2$ for $-1\le x\le1$, and $1$ for $x\ge1$. Find $P(X^2\le0.25)$.
Single correct (MCQ).
$P(X^2\le0.25)=P(-0.5\le X\le0.5)=F(0.5)-F(-0.5)=\tfrac14(1.5)^2-\tfrac14(0.5)^2=0.5$.
GATE DA 2025 Central Limit Theorem MCQ 2 marks
Q40. Let $Y=\sum_{i=1}^{300}X_i$ with $X_i\sim\text{Bernoulli}(0.25)$ i.i.d. Using the CLT ($\Phi$ = standard normal CDF), $P(60\le Y\le90)\approx$
Single correct (MCQ).
Mean $=300(0.25)=75$, variance $=300(0.25)(0.75)=56.25$, SD $=7.5$. $z=\pm\tfrac{15}{7.5}=\pm2$, giving $\Phi(2)-\Phi(-2)$.
GATE DA 2025 Random variables MCQ 2 marks
Q41. Let $Y=\lfloor X\rfloor$ where $X$ is exponential with mean $\tfrac{1}{\ln 10}$. For a positive integer $\ell$, $P(Y=\ell)=q^{\ell}(1-q)$. Find $q$.
Single correct (MCQ).
$\lambda=\ln 10$, so $P(Y=\ell)=e^{-\lambda\ell}(1-e^{-\lambda})=(0.1)^{\ell}(0.9)$. Hence $q=0.1$.

GATE DA 2025 Sample statistics MSQ 2 marks
Q54. A coin shows heads with probability $p$. With $X_i=1$ for head (else $0$) over $n$ independent tosses, let $\hat p=\tfrac1n\sum_{i=1}^n X_i$. Which is/are correct?
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
$E[\hat p]=p$ (A). $\operatorname{Var}(\hat p)=\tfrac{p(1-p)}{n}$, which decreases with $n$ (C). B and D are false.
GATE DA 2025 Expectation NAT 2 marks
Q61. A bag has $5$ white and $10$ black balls. $n$ balls are drawn one at a time with replacement; $S_n$ is the number of black balls drawn. Find $E[S_{100}]$ (1 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
Each draw is black with probability $\tfrac{10}{15}=\tfrac23$, so $E[S_{100}]=100\cdot\tfrac23\approx66.7$.
GATE DA 2024 Distributions MCQ 1 mark
Q11. Consider the statements: (i) the mean and variance of a Poisson random variable are equal; (ii) for a standard normal random variable, the mean is zero and the variance is one. Which ONE is correct?
Single correct (MCQ).
Poisson$(\lambda)$ has mean $=$ variance $=\lambda$; the standard normal is $\mathcal{N}(0,1)$. Both statements hold.
GATE DA 2024 Probability MCQ 1 mark
Q12. Three fair coins are tossed independently. $T$ is the event of two or more heads; $S$ is the event of two or more tails. What is $P(T\cap S)$?
Single correct (MCQ).
With only three tosses you cannot get $\ge2$ heads and $\ge2$ tails at once, so $T\cap S=\varnothing$ and $P=0$.
GATE DA 2024 Conditional independence MCQ 1 mark
Q24. Five random variables have joint distribution $P(V,W,X,Y,Z)=P(V)\,P(W)\,P(X\mid V,W)\,P(Y\mid X)\,P(Z\mid X)$. Which ONE statement is FALSE?
Single correct (MCQ).
$V$ and $W$ are parents of the common child $X$ (a collider); conditioning on $X$ makes them dependent, so C is false. $X$ separates the others, so A, B, D are true.

GATE DA 2024 Expectation MCQ 2 marks
Q36. A fair six-sided die is thrown repeatedly and independently. What is the expected number of throws until two consecutive even numbers appear?
Single correct (MCQ).
With $p=P(\text{even})=\tfrac12$, the expected wait for two consecutive successes is $\dfrac1p+\dfrac1{p^2}=2+4=6$.
GATE DA 2024 Random variables NAT 2 marks
Q56. Let $X\sim\text{Uniform}[1,3]$ and $Y\sim\text{Uniform}[2,4]$ be independent. The probability $P(X\ge Y)$ is ______ (3 dp).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.

Q65. Two fair coins are tossed independently. $X=1$ if both tosses are heads (else $0$); $Y=1$ if at least one toss is heads (else $0$). The covariance of $X$ and $Y$ is ______ (3 dp).

Practice Set Set theory of events MCQ 2 marks
P1. Consider three events $A,B,C$ such that $A\cap B\cap C=A$. Which conclusion follows necessarily (no extra assumptions)?
Single correct (MCQ).
$A\cap B\cap C\subseteq A$ always; equality forces every element of $A$ to be in both $B$ and $C$, i.e. $A\subseteq B$ and $A\subseteq C$. Strict subset isn't guaranteed since $A$ could equal $B$ or $C$.
Practice Set Random variables & CDFs MCQ 2 marks
P2. A random variable $X$ has CDF $P[X\le x]=0$ for $x<0$; $=x$ for $0\le x\le1$; $=1$ for $x>1$. Select the correct option.
Single correct (MCQ).
$F$ is non-decreasing, goes from $0$ to $1$, and is continuous — a valid CDF. $F(x)=x$ on $[0,1]$ is exactly the CDF of Uniform$(0,1)$.

P3. A continuous random variable $X$ has pdf $f(x)=\tfrac12x$. A valid support for $X$ is:

Practice Set Binomial distribution MSQ 2 marks
P4. A person buys one ticket in each of 10 independent lotteries, each with win probability $0.001$. Let $X$ = number of wins, $Y$ = number of non-wins. Select the correct option(s).
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
$X$ (wins) $\sim$ Binomial$(10,0.001)$ directly. $Y=10-X$ (non-wins) counts the "non-win" event with probability $0.999$, so $Y\sim$ Binomial$(10,0.999)$.
Practice Set Hypothesis testing MSQ 2 marks
P5. A two-sided t-test at $\alpha=0.05$ for $H_0:\mu=50$ vs $H_1:\mu\ne50$ gives a p-value of $0.03$. Which statement(s) are correct?
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
Reject $H_0$ if p-value $<\alpha$; here $0.03<0.05$, so $H_0$ is rejected. Converting to one-sided after seeing results is invalid, "accepted" is backwards logic, and Type II errors only apply when $H_0$ is *not* rejected.

P6. In a city, 40% are male, 60% female. 50% of males smoke; 40% of the whole population smokes. Given a person is a smoker, what is the probability they are male? (2 decimals.)
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
Total probability: $0.4=(0.5)(0.4)+P(\text{Smoke}|F)(0.6)\Rightarrow P(\text{Smoke}|F)=1/3$. Bayes: $P(M|\text{Smoke})=\dfrac{0.5\times0.4}{0.4}=0.5$.
Practice Set Inclusion-exclusion NAT 2 marks
P7. Events $A,B,C$ with $C\subset B$: $P(A)=0.6$, $P(B)=0.3$, $P(C)=0.05$, $P(A\cap B)=0.2$, $P(A\cap C)=0$, $P(B\cap C)=0.05$. Find $P(A\cup B\cup C)$ (2 decimals).
Numerical answer (NAT) — type a value and press Check.
Since $C\subset B$, $P(A\cap B\cap C)=P(A\cap C)=0$. Inclusion-exclusion: $0.6+0.3+0.05-0.2-0-0.05+0=0.70$.
Practice Set Hypothesis test selection MCQ 2 marks
P8. Which situation most appropriately requires an F-test?
Single correct (MCQ).
The F-test compares two variances via the ratio of sample variances (F-distribution). Known-variance mean tests use Z, small-sample mean tests use t, categorical independence uses chi-square.
Practice Set Conditioning & law of total probability MSQ 3 marks
P9. Two urns $U_1,U_2$ each contain red (R) and blue (B) balls. You pick one urn at random, then one ball. Which is/are always True?
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
$U_1,U_2$ are mutually exclusive and exhaustive, so for any event $E$, $P(U_1|E)+P(U_2|E)=1$ — true for $E=R$ and $E=B$. The reversed-conditioning sums (B, D) are proportions of two unrelated urns and need not add to 1.
Practice Set Skewness MSQ 1 mark
P10. Select the correct option(s). Usually, which of the following hold?
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
Right-skew (long right tail) pulls the mean rightmost: Mean > Median > Mode. Left-skew pulls the mean leftmost: Mean < Median < Mode. Options A and B state the reversed (wrong) pattern.
Practice Set Independence vs. mutual exclusivity MCQ 2 marks
P11. $A,B$ are mutually exclusive with $P(A)>0$, $P(B)>0$. Then,
Single correct (MCQ).
Mutually exclusive means $P(A\cap B)=0$. Independence requires $P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)>0$ (since both probabilities are positive). $0$ can never equal a positive number, so independence is impossible.
Practice Set Correlation: Pearson vs. Spearman MSQ 2 marks
P12. Let $r_{xy}$ be Pearson's correlation and $r_S$ Spearman's rank correlation. Select the correct option(s).
Multiple correct (MSQ) — select all that apply.
A high $|r_{xy}|$ does indicate linearity (A), and a high $|r_S|$ does indicate monotonicity (C). But the converses are false: a true linear relationship can still show low $r_{xy}$ under noise (B false), and low $r_S$ doesn't rule out a weak/noisy monotonic trend (D false).
Practice Set Covariance matrix MCQ 1 mark
P13. True or False: If a dataset has $n$ features, the variance-covariance matrix is $n\times n$.
Single correct (MCQ).
A covariance matrix $\Sigma$ has one row/column per feature: diagonal entries are variances, off-diagonal entries are covariances. With $n$ features, $\Sigma$ is necessarily $n\times n$.
Practice Set Binomial & combinatorics MCQ 3 marks
P14. A fair coin is tossed $2n$ times independently. The probability of an equal number of heads and tails is:
Single correct (MCQ).
Heads count $\sim$ Binomial$(2n,\tfrac12)$. "Equal heads & tails" means exactly $n$ heads: $P(X=n)=\binom{2n}{n}(\tfrac12)^n(\tfrac12)^n=\binom{2n}{n}/2^{2n}$.
