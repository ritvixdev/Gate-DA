Intro to Probability

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. A fair die is rolled. $P(\text{even})=?$
Even $=\{2,4,6\}$ is 3 of 6, not 2 of 6.
✅ Correct — $\{2,4,6\}$ is half the outcomes.
$\tfrac16$ is one face, not "even".
Too high — only 3 of 6 are even.
Q2. $P(A)=0.6,\ P(B)=0.5,\ P(A\cap B)=0.3$. $P(A\cup B)=?$
That's $P(A)+P(B)$ — subtract the overlap.
That's the intersection only.
✅ Correct — $0.6+0.5-0.3=0.8$.
Subtract the full overlap $0.3$ → $0.8$.
Q3. Two fair coins. $P(\text{at least one head})=?$
Use the complement: $1-P(\text{no heads})$.
That's $P(\text{no heads})=\tfrac14$, the opposite.
✅ Correct — $1-\tfrac14=\tfrac34$.
TT is possible, so it's not certain.
Q4. Two fair dice. $P(\text{sum}=7)=?$
There are 6 ways out of 36, not 3.
That's the chance of one specific pair.
✅ Correct — 6 favorable of 36 → $\tfrac{6}{36}=\tfrac16$.
7 is the sum, not the count of ways.
Q5. Two disjoint events with positive probability are…
No — knowing one occurred forces the other to not occur.
✅ Correct — disjoint events are dependent (mutually exclusive $\ne$ independent).
Disjoint means they share no outcomes.
They each have positive probability, so they're possible.
Q6. If $P(A)=0.3$, then $P(A^c)=?$
That's $P(A)$ itself.
The complement isn't zero unless $P(A)=1$.
✅ Correct — $P(A^c)=1-0.3=0.7$.
Probabilities can't exceed 1.
Q7. Which value can NOT be a probability?
$0$ is valid (impossible event).
Perfectly valid.
$1$ is valid (certain event).
✅ Correct — probabilities must lie in $[0,1]$.
Q8. One card from 52. $P(\text{King or Heart})=?$
That's $4+13$ — forgot to subtract the King of Hearts.
That's just hearts.
✅ Correct — $4+13-1=16$ (subtract the overlap).
That's just the kings.
