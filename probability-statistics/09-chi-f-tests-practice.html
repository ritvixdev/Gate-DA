Chi-square & F-test

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. The chi-square statistic is built from…
Means drive z/t tests.
✅ Correct — $\chi^2=\sum(O-E)^2/E$.
That's the F-test.
A different measure entirely.
Q2. Goodness-of-fit with 4 categories: df?
Subtract 1 from the category count.
That's for 2 categories.
✅ Correct — df $=k-1=3$.
That's $k^2$, unrelated.
Q3. A $3\times4$ contingency table: independence df?
That's $r\times c$, not the df.
That's $r+c$, not the df.
✅ Correct — $(3-1)(4-1)=6$.
That would be $rc-1$, the wrong rule here.
Q4. $s_1^2=20,\ s_2^2=5$. The F-statistic is…
Put the larger variance on top for $F\ge1$.
That's a sum, not a ratio.
✅ Correct — $F=20/5=4$.
That's the difference, not the ratio.
Q5. Chi-square uses…
Convert back to counts $O_i,E_i$.
✅ Correct — it compares observed and expected counts.
Means belong to z/t tests.
Not z-scores.
Q6. Chi-square and F tests reject in the…
Small values mean a good fit (don't reject).
✅ Correct — large statistics (upper tail) ⇒ reject.
These are right-tailed tests.
The center supports $H_0$.
Q7. Can $F$ be negative?
A ratio of variances can't be negative.
✅ Correct — variances are non-negative, so $F\ge0$.
Sample size doesn't allow negatives.
Never negative, in ANOVA or otherwise.
Q8. The F-test is the engine behind…
Bayes is about conditional probability.
The CLT is a separate theorem.
✅ Correct — ANOVA uses an F-ratio to compare group means.
Correlation isn't an F-test.
