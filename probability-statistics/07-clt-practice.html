Central Limit Theorem

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. The standard error of $\bar X$ is…
That's the population std; the mean's spread is smaller.
That's the variance of $\bar X$; SE is its root.
✅ Correct — SE $=\sigma/\sqrt n$.
Spread shrinks with $n$, not grows.
Q2. $\sigma=10,\ n=100$. Standard error?
That's $\sigma$; divide by $\sqrt{100}$.
✅ Correct — $10/\sqrt{100}=1$.
You divided by $n$, not $\sqrt n$.
SE shrinks, it doesn't grow with $n$.
Q3. To halve the standard error you need…
Doubling $n$ only divides SE by $\sqrt2$.
That increases SE.
✅ Correct — SE $\propto1/\sqrt n$, so $4n$ halves it.
Then SE stays the same.
Q4. The CLT makes which thing approximately normal?
The raw data keeps its own shape.
✅ Correct — the distribution of $\bar X$ becomes normal.
The population is whatever it is.
The CLT is about the mean, not the variance.
Q5. Does the CLT require a normal population?
No — that's the whole power of the CLT.
✅ Correct — almost any population works for large $n$.
Larger $n$ is exactly when it kicks in.
It applies to continuous data too.
Q6. If the population is already Normal, $\bar X$ is Normal for…
No threshold needed when the population is Normal.
It's exactly Normal.
✅ Correct — averaging Normals gives a Normal for any $n$.
It holds for all $n$, not just 1.
Q7. The mean of the sampling distribution of $\bar X$ is…
The mean doesn't shrink — only the spread does.
✅ Correct — $\bar X$ is centered at the population mean $\mu$.
Only if $\mu=0$.
It's $\mu$, not scaled by $n$.
Q8. Mean of 36 dice rolls: SE $\approx?$ ($\sigma\approx1.71$)
That's $\sigma$; divide by $\sqrt{36}=6$.
You divided by $n$, not $\sqrt n$.
✅ Correct — $1.71/6\approx0.285$.
SE shrinks; it can't exceed $\sigma$.
