Random Variables

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. $X$: $P(X=0)=0.5,\ P(X=2)=0.5$. $E[X]=?$
It's a weighted average, not the minimum.
✅ Correct — $0(0.5)+2(0.5)=1$.
That's the max value, not the mean.
That's a probability, not the mean.
Q2. Same $X$. $\operatorname{Var}(X)=?$
Variance is 0 only if $X$ is constant.
That's $E[X^2]$, before subtracting $(E[X])^2$.
✅ Correct — $E[X^2]=2$, $\operatorname{Var}=2-1^2=1$.
That's $E[X^2]$ of the value 2 alone.
Q3. $\operatorname{Var}(X)=4$. $\operatorname{Var}(3X+5)=?$
That's $3\times4$; variance scales by $a^2$.
The $+5$ doesn't add to variance.
✅ Correct — $3^2\cdot4=36$ (the $+5$ drops out).
Don't add the constant; just scale by $9$.
Q4. The fast formula for variance is…
Sign flipped — this would be negative.
✅ Correct — $\operatorname{Var}(X)=E[X^2]-(E[X])^2$.
It's a difference, not a sum.
Units don't even match.
Q5. $E[X+Y]$ equals $E[X]+E[Y]$…
Expectation is linear regardless of dependence.
It always holds.
✅ Correct — expectation is always linear (independence isn't needed).
No such condition is required.
Q6. $\operatorname{Var}(X+Y)=\operatorname{Var}(X)+\operatorname{Var}(Y)$ requires…
In general there's a $+2\operatorname{Cov}$ term.
✅ Correct — variances add only when the covariance is zero.
Means are irrelevant here.
If $X=Y$ the covariance is large, not zero.
Q7. A fair die: $E[X]=?$
Close, but the mean of 1–6 is $3.5$.
That's the max face.
✅ Correct — $(1+\dots+6)/6=21/6=3.5$.
That's the sum, not the average.
Q8. Which is always true?
Equal only when variance is 0.
✅ Correct — since $\operatorname{Var}=E[X^2]-(E[X])^2\ge0$.
Variance is never negative.
It scales by $a^2$, not $a$.
