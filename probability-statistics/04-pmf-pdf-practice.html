PMF, PDF & Distributions

8 questions. Tap any option — you'll see why it's right or wrong, and the correct one lights up.

Q1. For a continuous $X$, $P(X=2)=?$
$f(2)$ is a density, not a probability.
✅ Correct — single points have probability 0; only intervals count.
No single point has probability 1.
$F(2)=P(X\le2)$, not $P(X=2)$.
Q2. Binomial$(n,p)$ mean and variance?
Variance carries the extra $(1-p)$ factor.
Those are Bernoulli (single trial).
✅ Correct — mean $np$, variance $np(1-p)$.
Mean and variance are swapped.
Q3. Poisson$(\lambda)$: mean and variance?
Both equal $\lambda$.
Both equal $\lambda$.
✅ Correct — Poisson has mean = variance = $\lambda$.
Those are the Exponential's.
Q4. About what % of a Normal lies within $\mu\pm2\sigma$?
68% is within $\mu\pm1\sigma$.
99.7% is within $\mu\pm3\sigma$.
✅ Correct — ~95% within two standard deviations.
50% is just one side of the mean.
Q5. To use a Normal table you standardize with…
You must also divide by $\sigma$.
✅ Correct — $Z=(X-\mu)/\sigma\sim\mathcal N(0,1)$.
Subtract the mean first.
Divide by $\sigma$, don't multiply.
Q6. The CDF $F(x)$ represents…
That's the PMF for discrete $X$.
✅ Correct — the CDF accumulates probability up to $x$.
That's the PDF $f(x)$.
Unrelated to a single summary number.
Q7. Binomial → Poisson approximation holds when…
It's the opposite regime.
Not a meaningful condition.
✅ Correct — rare events over many trials, with $\lambda=np$.
It's a standard, useful approximation.
Q8. A PDF must satisfy…
A density can exceed 1; only the area is bounded.
Sums are for PMFs; PDFs integrate.
✅ Correct — non-negative with $\int f=1$.
It's a density; probability is the area under it.
